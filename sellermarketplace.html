The 925 Standard

Sterling silver is an alloy of 92.5% pure silver and 7.5% other metals (typically copper), making it both workable and durable. The 925 hallmark is the international guarantee of this composition. All pieces on SellerMarketplace meet or exceed this standard and are independently verified before listing.

Aftercare

Care & Polish

How to keep your sterling silver looking beautiful for years to come.

Understanding Tarnish

Sterling silver naturally tarnishes over time when exposed to air, moisture, and certain chemicals. This is a completely normal chemical reaction — silver reacts with sulfur compounds in the air to form silver sulfide. Tarnish is not damage; it is easily removed and does not affect the integrity of the silver.

Daily Care Tips

  • Put jewelry on last when getting ready — after applying perfume, lotions, and hairspray.
  • Remove silver before swimming, bathing, or using cleaning products. Chlorine accelerates tarnishing.
  • Wipe pieces with a soft, dry cloth after wearing to remove skin oils and residue.
  • Remove rings before washing hands or doing dishes whenever possible.
  • Avoid wearing silver jewelry during exercise — perspiration accelerates tarnish.

Cleaning Your Silver

○

Polishing Cloth

For light tarnish, a silver polishing cloth (included with every order) is all you need. Gentle circular motions restore shine in minutes.

◇

Soap & Water

For a deeper clean, use mild dish soap in warm water. Gently scrub with a soft toothbrush, rinse thoroughly, and dry completely.

△

Silver Polish

For heavy tarnish, a specialized silver polish cream applied with a soft cloth will restore the original shine.

⬡

Avoid

Never use bleach, toothpaste, or ultrasonic cleaners on silver with stones or intricate detailing.

Storage

  • Store silver in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and humidity.
  • Keep pieces in individual pouches or compartments to prevent scratching.
  • Anti-tarnish strips placed in your jewelry box will significantly slow tarnish development.
  • Avoid storing silver in rubber or wool — both contain sulfur compounds that accelerate tarnish.
